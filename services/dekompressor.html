Dekompressor® discectomy

What is Dekompressor® discectomy?

The Dekompressor® Discectomy is an advanced, minimally invasive treatment designed for patients suffering from chronic back or leg pain caused by a herniated disc that has not improved with conservative care, such as physical therapy, chiropractic adjustments, or epidural injections. Unlike traditional "open" spine surgery, which requires large incisions and bone removal, the Dekompressor® procedure uses a tiny, specialized probe to remove a small amount of the disc's interior. This creates space within the disc, allowing the "bulge" to pull back in and significantly reducing the pressure on your spinal nerves.

At Goodlife, your safety, comfort, and long-term recovery are our primary goals. Our experienced medical team carefully reviews your MRI to ensure the Dekompressor® is the right tool for your specific disc herniation. We take the time to explain how this "mechanical decompression" works to heal the disc from the inside out. You're never pressured-only supported with honest guidance and a personalized plan to help you return to your daily life. If a herniated disc has been limiting your mobility and quality of life, we invite you to schedule a consultation to see if this advanced, non-surgical option is right for you.

Right for You?

Dekompressor® discectomy could be right for you if you experience any of the following:

  • Persistent pain that hasn't improved with conservative care
  • Herniated or bulging discs confirmed by an MRI
  • Sciatica or sharp, radiating pain that travels down the leg
  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness in the lower extremities

Potential Benefits

Pevious patients who recieved Dekompressor® discectomy have seen:

  • It may delay or prevent the need for surgery
  • Provides longer-lasting pain relief (often months or more)
  • Targets pain at its source without surgery
  • Improves mobility and daily function
  • Reduces reliance on pain medications
  • Minimally invasive with short recovery time
  • Repeatable if pain returns over time
